A short checklist that keeps models maintainable and your components robust.

## Do

- **`defaultInjectionStrategy = OPTIONAL`** — never let a missing authored property fail the whole component; handle null/`@Default`.
- **Adapt from `Resource`** unless you truly need request data — it stays exportable and testable.
- **One model per component**, named after it; expose plain getters that HTL reads.
- **Compute in `@PostConstruct`**, not in getters — getters may be called many times per render.
- **Program to interfaces** for injected services (`@OSGiService` by interface).
- **Use specific injectors** (`@ValueMapValue`, `@ChildResource`) over the generic `@Inject`.
- **Return safe empties** — an empty `List`, not `null`, so HTL `data-sly-list` doesn't blow up.

## Avoid

- Business logic or repository writes inside a model — models are for **reading/shaping** content for the view. Writes belong in services/servlets.
- `SlingHttpServletRequest` adaptable "just in case".
- Heavy work (HTTP calls, queries) in a model that renders on every request — cache it in a service.
- Catching and swallowing exceptions silently — log with context.

## A model you can unit-test

Because a model is a plain class, a test just adapts a mock resource and asserts getters — no running
AEM. (That's the next module, *Unit Testing*.)

<Callout type="do">If a model is hard to test, it's usually doing too much. Move side effects and I/O into a service, keep the model a thin, null-safe view of the content, and your HTL stays a dumb template.</Callout>
