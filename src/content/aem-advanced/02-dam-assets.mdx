The **Digital Asset Management** (DAM) console — `/assets.html/content/dam` — stores images, video and
documents, plus the **metadata** AEM extracts from them.

## Uploading & metadata

Upload an image to the DAM and open it: AEM has automatically extracted its **metadata**, including
**camera data** (EXIF — camera model, exposure, GPS, …) when the original photo carries it.

<Callout type="note">Two images can look alike but carry very different metadata: a shot straight from a camera keeps full EXIF, while one exported or processed may have it stripped. Open both and compare the *Camera Data* to see the difference.</Callout>

## Renditions — why the published image is smaller

Add a DAM image to a page with the **Image** component, publish, and the file visitors download is
usually **smaller** than the original you uploaded. AEM serves an optimised **rendition**, not the raw
asset:

- On ingestion, DAM workflows generate **renditions** — resized, re-encoded copies — alongside the original.
- The Core Image component requests an appropriately sized, web-optimised rendition (often WebP) for the viewport.

Try it: add an asset to `/content/academy/language-master/en`, publish, then **Save image as** from the
published page and compare its size to the original — the delta is the rendition doing its job.

<Callout type="do">Upload the **highest-quality original** and let AEM generate the optimised renditions. Never hand-shrink images before upload — you throw away quality the renditions could have used.</Callout>
