GitHub Copilot is an **AI pair programmer** built into your editor. It doesn't "know" your project —
it predicts the most likely next code given the **context it can see** (your open files, selection,
and what you tell it). Treat it as a fast, well-read junior who needs review, not an oracle.

## The three ways you'll use it

<Callout type="note">These overlap, but it helps to know which one you're invoking — each gets a different amount of context and control.</Callout>

| Mode | What it does | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Completions** (ghost text) | Suggests the next line(s) as you type | Boilerplate, repetitive code, the "obvious" next step |
| **Chat** | A conversation in a side panel or inline | Explaining, debugging, generating a block, asking "how do I…" |
| **Edits / Agent** | Multi-file changes from a single instruction | Refactors, "add a field to this component end-to-end" |

## How it actually works (the mental model)

Copilot sends a **prompt** — assembled from your nearby code, open tabs, and your message — to a
large language model, which returns the statistically likely continuation. Two consequences follow
that shape everything in this module:

- **Context quality = output quality.** Garbage or missing context → confident but wrong code.
- **It predicts plausible, not correct.** It will happily invent an API method that doesn't exist
  (a *hallucination*). That's why AEM — with its niche APIs — needs extra care (lessons 05–06).

<Callout type="warn">Copilot does not run your code, read your runtime, or check AEM's Javadoc. A suggestion that *looks* right can reference a method that was removed, a Granite resource type that doesn't exist, or an HTL block option that was never valid. **You** are the compiler-of-last-resort.</Callout>

## The one habit that makes Copilot safe

**Read every suggestion before you accept it.** The developers who get burned are the ones who
tab-accept on autopilot; the ones who get a real speed-up read the diff, keep what's right, and
re-prompt for the rest. We'll build that habit throughout the module.

<Callout type="do">Think of Copilot as **draft-generation + review**, never **generate-and-ship**. Your value shifts from typing to specifying and verifying.</Callout>
